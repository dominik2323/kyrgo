## Part 1: Bishkek → Son-Kul

Getting south through the guts of Kyrgyzstan. Soviet ghosts, mountain passes, villages that time forgot. The route through Kochkor is the tourist way — there might be alternatives worth exploring.

### Urbexes

**Min-Kush**
Soviet uranium ghost town. Population dropped from 20k to 2k. Decaying apartment blocks, massive Lenin statue, cultural center with preserved murals, abandoned school and hospital. Haunting. Some radiation in mining areas — don't lick the walls.

### Soviet Stuff & Monuments

**Kochkor Lenin Statue**
Silver-painted Lenin in front of regional government. One of many still standing across Kyrgyzstan.

Soviet propaganda murals on cultural center and former cinema. Slowly returning to nature.
- 41.6833, 74.4667 — [Open in Maps](https://www.google.com/maps?q=41.6833,74.4667)

### Food (No Tourist Traps)

**Dungan Ashlyam-fu Shack (Kochkor)**
One-dish spot run by Dungan family. Homemade cold spicy noodle soup. The real thing. Ask locals in Kochkor.
- [Explorer Genes](https://explorergenes.com/the-surprising-little-stop-of-kochkor-kyrgyzstan/)

**Cafe Bayan (Kochkor)**
Local spot with ashlyam-fu and boso lagman.
- [Evendo](https://evendo.com/locations/kyrgyzstan/kochkor/best-restaurants)

**CBT Homestays**
Best Kyrgyz food is homemade. CBT arranges homestays where families cook traditional meals.
- 42.2131, 75.7575 — [Open in Maps](https://www.google.com/maps?q=42.2131,75.7575)
- [CBT Kyrgyzstan](https://cbtkyrgyzstan.com/)

### Worth a Stop

**Burana Tower**
11th century minaret, 25m tall (was 45m before earthquakes). Balbals (ancient gravestones) collection nearby. Small museum. Climb to top for mountain views. 150 som entry.
- 42.7463, 75.2502 — [Open in Maps](https://www.google.com/maps?q=42.7463,75.2502)
- [Central Asia Guide](https://central-asia.guide/kyrgyzstan/destinations-kg/bishkek/burana-tower/)

**Konorchek Canyon**
Red sandstone formations like mini Grand Canyon. Free entry. Bring water — desert dry.
- 42.45, 75.90 — [Open in Maps](https://www.google.com/maps?q=42.45,75.90)
- [Megan Starr](https://www.meganstarr.com/konorchek-canyon-red-bridge-kyrgyzstan/)

## Part 2: Son-Kul → Issyk-Kul

South shore of Issyk-Kul has the red mountains and scenic canyons. Son-Kul itself is the high-altitude nomad experience. Route between them crosses the Kalmak-Ashuu Pass (3,440m) — one of the most beautiful drives in the country.

### Urbexes

**Kadji-Sai Uranium Mine**
Soviet uranium mine and processing plant (1949-1967). Derelict infrastructure looms over the beach village. Environmental disaster in slow motion — tailings wash into Issyk-Kul when it rains. Don't swim nearby. Remediation starting 2025.
- 42.17, 77.23 — [Open in Maps](https://www.google.com/maps?q=42.17,77.23)
- [Eurasianet](https://eurasianet.org/kyrgyzstan-abandoned-uranium-mine-looms-over-tranquil-beach-haven)

### Soviet Stuff & Monuments

**Gagarin Bust (Barskoon)**
Concrete bust of Yuri Gagarin at the waterfall trailhead. He vacationed on Issyk-Kul's south shore after his spaceflight. Soviet lorry mounted on plinth nearby.
- 42.002, 77.577 — [Open in Maps](https://www.google.com/maps?q=42.002,77.577)
- [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barskoon_Waterfall)

**Jeti-Oguz Sanatorium**
Soviet-era resort with healing geothermal springs at 2200m. Still operating. Brutalist vibes meet hot water.
- 42.3167, 77.7833 — [Open in Maps](https://www.google.com/maps?q=42.3167,77.7833)
- [Central Asia Guide](https://central-asia.guide/kyrgyzstan/destinations-kg/issyk-kul/jeti-oguz/)

### Food (No Tourist Traps)

**Bokonbayevo homestays**
CBT-organized homestays in the village near Skazka Canyon. Traditional meals with families.
- 42.1172, 76.9931 — [Open in Maps](https://www.google.com/maps?q=42.1172,76.9931)
- [CBT Kyrgyzstan](https://cbtkyrgyzstan.com/)

**Yurt camps at Son-Kul**
Nomad families cook traditional food. Kymyz (fermented mare's milk), beshbarmak, fresh bread. The real deal.
- 41.85, 75.15 — [Open in Maps](https://www.google.com/maps?q=41.85,75.15)
- [Horizon Guides](https://horizonguides.com/trips/ecotour-kg-sonkul-issykul)

### Worth a Stop

**Skazka Canyon**
Martian landscape on Issyk-Kul's south shore. Red sandstone formations eroded into castles and dragons. Best at sunset. 50 som entry.
- 42.1607, 77.3309 — [Open in Maps](https://www.google.com/maps?q=42.1607,77.3309)
- [The Planet Edit](https://www.theplanetedit.com/skazka-canyon-kyrgyzstan/)

**Jeti-Oguz (Seven Bulls)**
Iconic red rock fins rising from green hills. "Broken Heart" rock across the road. Legend involves a khan, a woman, and seven bulls.
- 42.3167, 77.7833 — [Open in Maps](https://www.google.com/maps?q=42.3167,77.7833)
- [Zig Zag on Earth](https://www.zigzagonearth.com/jeti-oguz-kyrgyzstan/)

**Kalmak-Ashuu Pass**
3,440m pass between Son-Kul and Issyk-Kul. Summer pastures, yurts, cattle, mountains. One of Kyrgyzstan's best drives.
- 41.95, 75.85 — [Open in Maps](https://www.google.com/maps?q=41.95,75.85)
- [Horizon Guides](https://horizonguides.com/trips/ecotour-kg-sonkul-issykul)

**Orto-Tokoy Reservoir**
Small turquoise lake shortly after leaving Issyk-Kul heading to Son-Kul. Quick photo stop.
- 42.15, 75.75 — [Open in Maps](https://www.google.com/maps?q=42.15,75.75)
- [Zig Zag on Earth](https://www.zigzagonearth.com/issyk-kul-to-song-kul-kyrgyzstan/)

## Part 3: Issyk-Kul → Bishkek

Final stretch. Karakol is the adventure hub — Dungan culture, best food in Kyrgyzstan, gateway to alpine treks. North shore has Soviet sanatoriums and ancient petroglyphs. Then back through the mountains to Bishkek.

### Urbexes

**Abandoned Young Pioneer Camp**
Massive Soviet summer camp left to rot since 1991. Near Bel-Tam on south shore. Boarded windows, crumbling walkways, dead birds in classrooms. Most doors locked but windows are... flexible. Labelled "Pionerskiy Lager' Zor'ka" on maps.
- 42.15, 77.10 — [Open in Maps](https://www.google.com/maps?q=42.15,77.10)
- [Travel Tramp](https://www.travel-tramp.com/exploring-an-abandoned-soviet-young-pioneer-summer-camp-in-kyrgyzstan/)

**Barskoon Pioneer Camp**
Another abandoned camp on south shore. Still has a Lenin monument standing.
- 42.10, 77.55 — [Open in Maps](https://www.google.com/maps?q=42.10,77.55)
- [Alamy photos](https://www.alamy.com/stock-photo-lenin-monument-in-an-abandoned-young-pioneer-camp-in-barskoon-on-the-47785926.html)

### Soviet Stuff & Monuments

**Aurora Sanatorium**
Ship-shaped brutalist sanatorium built 1979 for Communist Party elite. Still operating. 350 employees once served 200 guests. Looks like a beached Soviet cruise liner.
- 42.67, 77.33 — [Open in Maps](https://www.google.com/maps?q=42.67,77.33)
- [Live the World](https://www.livetheworld.com/activities/kyrgyzstan/aurora-sanatorium-cholpon-ata)
- [Lyuba Tours](https://lyubatours.com/blog/aurora-sanatorium)

**Issyk-Ata Sanatorium**
Near Bishkek. One of Central Asia's oldest. WW2 soldier treatment center. Mass grave on overlooking hill. Still treats radiation illness from Chernobyl.
- 42.60, 75.00 — [Open in Maps](https://www.google.com/maps?q=42.60,75.00)
- [Megan Starr](https://www.meganstarr.com/issyk-ata-sanatorium/)
- [New East Archive](https://www.new-east-archive.org/features/show/11434/the-escapist-issyk-ata-sanatorium-kyrgyzstan)

**Cholpon-Ata Petroglyphs**
42 hectares of Bronze Age rock carvings. 5,000+ stones. Hunting scenes, sun worship, chariots. Best light early morning or late afternoon. 800m-3km walking routes.
- 42.65, 77.08 — [Open in Maps](https://www.google.com/maps?q=42.65,77.08)
- [Zig Zag on Earth](https://www.zigzagonearth.com/kyrgyzstan-cholpon-ata-petroglyphs/)
- [Advantour](https://www.advantour.com/kyrgyzstan/cholpon-ata-petroglyphs.htm)

**Dungan Mosque (Karakol)**
Chinese Buddhist temple that's actually a mosque. Built 1907-1910 entirely of wood with zero nails. Dragons, phoenixes, pagoda minaret. Dungan refugees from China built it.
- 42.4907, 78.3936 — [Open in Maps](https://www.google.com/maps?q=42.4907,78.3936)
- [Atlas Obscura](https://www.atlasobscura.com/places/dungan-mosque)
- [Advantour](https://www.advantour.com/kyrgyzstan/karakol/karakol-dungan-mosque.htm)

### Food (No Tourist Traps)

**Ashlan-fu Alley (Karakol)**
Bogu Bazaar. Multiple stalls serving Karakol's signature dish. Saida's does 600 bowls/day at 30 som (~$0.35). Cold, spicy, vinegary noodle soup. Proper hangover cure.
- 42.4907, 78.3936 — [Open in Maps](https://www.google.com/maps?q=42.4907,78.3936)
- [Atlas Obscura](https://www.atlasobscura.com/articles/what-to-eat-kyrgyzstan)
- [Destination Karakol](https://destinationkarakol.com/food-drink/kyrgyz-food/)

**Dungan Family Dinner**
Book through Destination Karakol. Home-cooked ashlan-fu, ganfan, tomomo desserts. ~$17/person with transport. Minimum 4 people.
- [Destination Karakol](https://destinationkarakol.com/food-drink/kyrgyz-food/)

**Uyghur Home Dinner**
Rare dishes like goshnan (meat pie) you can't get in restaurants. Through Destination Karakol.
- [Adventures of Nicole](https://adventuresoflilnicki.com/karakol-where-to-eat/)

### Worth a Stop

**Altyn-Arashan Hot Springs**
Natural hot springs at end of 15km trek. Soviet-era huts, alpine meadows, views of snow-capped Palatka peak. Worth the walk.
- 42.43, 78.55 — [Open in Maps](https://www.google.com/maps?q=42.43,78.55)
- [See Her Travel](https://www.seehertravel.com/best-hiking-altyn-arashan-kyrgyzstan/)

**Karakol Town**
Melting pot — Kyrgyz, Dungan, Tatar, Uyghur, Russian. Best food scene in the country. Dungan mosque, Sunday animal market, gateway to mountains.
- 42.4907, 78.3936 — [Open in Maps](https://www.google.com/maps?q=42.4907,78.3936)
- [Backpack Adventures](https://www.backpackadventures.org/things-to-do-in-karakol-kyrgyzstan/)

**Jyrgalan Valley**
60km from Karakol. Former coal mining village turned adventure tourism hub. Less crowded trails, CBT community tourism. Good for hiking and horse treks.
- 42.507, 78.978 — [Open in Maps](https://www.google.com/maps?q=42.507,78.978)
- [Journal of Nomads](https://www.journalofnomads.com/hiking-in-kyrgyzstan-jyrgalan-karakol/)

## How This Works

**Not a schedule. A menu.**

Each section lists options. We look at the map, check the weather, see how we feel, pick something. Maybe we spend two days at one spot. Maybe we skip a whole section. The point is having options researched and ready, not following a script.

The best stuff usually isn't planned anyway.
