## Philosophy

> **"We live once. The best stories come from the edge - but the edge isn't where you die, it's where you feel alive."**

Fixed gear mentality — pedals always turning, no coasting, no hiding behind safety brakes. Commitment in mechanical form. You either learn to control it or you crash. Same with mountains, same with life. Be deliberate, stay engaged, trust your skills, don't be an idiot.

Punk spirit with mountain wisdom. Hate unnecessary bureaucracy, touristy bullshit, and overthinking until the adventure is gone. But not reckless — knowing exactly where the line is because you've danced on it.

Some danger is worth it, some isn't. Know the difference.

## The Trip

**April 3-12, 2025** | 10 days | Rental car + tent

### Route Overview
1. **Bishkek → Son-Kul** — Soviet ghosts, mountain passes, Min-Kush uranium ghost town
2. **Son-Kul → Issyk-Kul** — High plateau, nomads, red canyons, Skazka
3. **Issyk-Kul → Bishkek** — Karakol, Dungan food, abandoned pioneer camps, petroglyphs

Full details in [april-2025-roadmap.md](./trips-roadmap/april-2025-roadmap.md)

---

## Places to See

### Hikes
- Ala Archa National Park (day trips near Bishkek)
- Konorchek Canyon (red rock desert)
- Skazka Canyon (martian landscape)
- Jeti-Oguz Valley of Flowers
- Altyn-Arashan Hot Springs (15km trek)

### Urbex / Soviet Ruins
- **Min-Kush** — Uranium ghost town, 20k→2k population, Lenin statue, decaying Soviet blocks
- **Kadji-Sai** — Abandoned uranium mine overlooking Issyk-Kul
- **Pioneer Camps** — Rotting Soviet summer camps on south shore
- **Aurora Sanatorium** — Ship-shaped brutalist building for Communist elite

### Worth a Stop
- Burana Tower (11th century minaret)
- Cholpon-Ata Petroglyphs (Bronze Age rock carvings)
- Dungan Mosque in Karakol (wood, no nails, dragons)
- Son-Kul yurt camps (real nomads, not tourist setups)

## April Conditions

Shoulder season. Expect:
- Snow possible at altitude (Son-Kul can be frozen)
- Cold nights (below freezing in mountains)
- Fewer tourists
- Some passes may be closed or sketchy
- Unpredictable weather — always have warm layers accessible

---

## Planning Approach

**Not a schedule. A menu.**

Options researched and ready. Look at the map, check the weather, see how we feel, pick something. Maybe spend two days at one spot. Maybe skip a whole section.

The best stuff usually isn't planned anyway.
